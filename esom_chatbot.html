What is monetary policy?
Monetary policy refers to the actions undertaken by a nation's central bank to control the money supply and achieve sustainable economic growth. In India, the RBI implements monetary policy through tools like the repo rate, which influences interest rates across the economy.
Explain inflation targeting in India.
The Reserve Bank of India follows a flexible inflation targeting framework where it aims to maintain consumer price inflation at 4% with a tolerance band of +/- 2%. This approach helps balance price stability with economic growth objectives.
